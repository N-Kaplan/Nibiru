Reaching for the stars

Humans have ever looked up at the sky in wonder and yearning. The first artificial satellite was launched in 1957, and ever since we have been pushing the boundaries to reach the sky and beyond.

First people in space

On April 12 1961, Russian cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin was the first human in space, completing a single orbit around the Earth during his 108-minute flight. Humanity kept reaching for the stars, and on July 20, 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to reach the moon.

Different nations have kept exploring the solar system and beyond with unmanned missions, but manned missions remained limited to Earth Orbit for decades.

Space Tourism

The first steps into recreational space tourism were on various government-owned vehicles such as the Russian Soyuz and the International Space Station in 2001.

Various companies have started to offer entirely commercial space flights, up to 2021 still limited to Earth Orbit.

Nibiru: The Next Chapter

Niburu, established in 2021, envisions a future where touring our Solar System is available to the general populations.

Our mission is enabling anyone to experience the wonders of spaceflight and the amazing sights of our Solar System in person.
